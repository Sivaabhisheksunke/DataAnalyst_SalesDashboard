Sales Dashboards serve as a tool for understanding sales data. The Sales Dashboard displays the sales data in its changed and cleaned state, and it uses that work product to help create actionable and tactical insights on how the company is performing with respect to sales and possibly also utilizing the sales data to make decisions about future sales opportunities.

The user can utilize the Sales Dashboard to get a broad understanding of the material Key Performance Indicators (KPIs), such as **Total Sales, Total Profit, Profit Margin, Revenue Trends, Regional Performance, Growth Rate Analysis, and Top-Selling Products**. In addition, the user can utilize dynamic filters as well as dynamic visuals to analyze the results based on various dimensions, such as time, geography, product category, and customer group.

The ultimate purpose of the Sales Dashboard Project is to allow users (Stakeholders) to quickly identify patterns that exist with respect to the company's sales performance, assess how well the company is doing with respect to sales, monitor the rate at which the company is growing, and discover potential areas for optimization. This mirrors the way in which companies operate using historical data and the analysis of that data in a way that will help the company increase its revenue and execute its strategic plans to achieve future growth.
